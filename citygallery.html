Now you are getting to know more about the cities of Afghanistan


kabul

Kabul is the capital and largest city of Afghanistan, with a population of over 4 million and is the political, cultural and economic center of Afghanistan. The city has a rich history that dates back more than 3,500 years. Kabul, which is one of the most important cities in Afghanistan, is home to several important historical sites, including Darul Aman Palace, the tomb of Timur Shah Durrani, and the Paghman Gardens. Factories operating there include: the $25 million Habib Gulzar Factory, the official exclusive representative of Coca-Cola in Afghanistan, the official representative of Toyota Afghanistan, and the official representative of Nestle products in Afghanistan, the Alokozay Soft Drinks Factory, the Sher Qawt Factory, and…

Bamian

Bamyan is a historical city in the center of Afghanistan. The city was selected as the cultural capital of the SAARC member countries in 2015. The Bamyan idols and their surroundings located in the city were registered as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 2003. Traditional foods of the city include keshko (water curd), red halva, birio (made from wheat), qoruti (made from curd and bread), and leti.

Herat

Herat is one of the major cities of Afghanistan, the capital of Herat Province in the west of the country. It is the third most populous city in Afghanistan. The city is famous for its magnificent minarets and architecture. Among the historical places in Herat are the Herat Citadel, the Herat Mosque, the Herat Mosque Minarets, the Gohar Shad Tomb, and the Tomb of Khwaja Abdullah Ansari. Herat grapes are also world famous.

Mazar Sharif

Mazar-e-Sharif is the capital of Balkh Province in northern Afghanistan and the fourth largest city in Afghanistan after Kabul, Herat, and Kandahar. The Ali bin Abi Talib Mosque, also known as the Blue Mosque or Rawda Sharif or Ziarat Sakhi, is located in Mazar-e-Sharif in northern Balkh Province, Afghanistan. It is one of the most important historical monuments in Afghanistan and the center of the Nowruz celebration in northern Afghanistan. The Red Rose Festival (Mila Gul Sorkh) is a special celebration that is held in Mazar-e-Sharif during the first 3 days of the year, on the occasion of the blooming of red tulips on the slopes of the green plains overlooking the city. During these 3 days, people from all over come to Mazar-e-Sharif to attend this celebration. In Mazar-e-Sharif, Qabili Polo is one of the traditional and very popular dishes. Qabili Polo is a pilaf dish with meat (usually lamb) and thin rice, prepared with carrots, raisins, sliced ​​pistachios, and almonds. This dish has its roots in northern Afghanistan.
